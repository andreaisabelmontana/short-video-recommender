Short-Video Recommender

The endless feed is the most aggressive recommender ever built: one video at a time, judged by how long you lingered before the swipe. This build is that engine — learning from split-second watch signals to decide what plays next. I rebuilt it from scratch because it's recommendation under the hardest constraint: instant, implicit, and relentless.

What it is

One feed, judged in seconds

Unlike a store of products you browse, a short-video feed shows you exactly one thing and watches what you do. There are no stars and no reviews — just behaviour: did you watch to the end, replay it, like it, or flick it away in half a second? Those micro-signals are the entire training set, and the system has to turn them into the next pick, immediately.

The architecture mirrors how the real ones work: a fast candidate stage narrows millions of videos to a few hundred plausible ones, then a heavier ranking stage orders those by predicted watch-time. Splitting the problem this way is the only way to be both broad and fast.

Architecture

How the next video is chosen

Every swipe triggers the same two-stage decision:

  1. Observe

    Capture the watch signals from the video just shown.

  2. Generate

    Shortlist candidate videos that match the evolving interest profile.

  3. Rank

    Score the shortlist by predicted watch-time and engagement.

  4. Mix

    Blend in a little exploration so new interests can surface.

  5. Serve & learn

    Play the top pick and fold the next reaction straight back in.

Reflection

What rebuilding it taught me

  • Behaviour is the only label. With no ratings, watch-time and skips are the ground truth — reading them right is the whole job.
  • Two stages or bust. You can't deeply rank millions in real time; candidate-then-rank is what makes instant recommendation possible.
  • Sessions have momentum. What someone wants shifts minute to minute, so the model has to respect sequence, not just identity.
  • Exploration prevents collapse. Pure exploitation traps a user in a shrinking loop; deliberate exploration keeps the feed alive.
  • Optimising watch-time has ethics. The metric that works is also the one that can trap attention — worth building with eyes open.
